⚠️ Build Capacity Constraint — Contracts Hub Workflow Development
The Contracts Hub has a dedicated TPM who carries significant platform-level responsibilities spanning the full Hub ecosystem. However, the volume and pace of individual workflow builds has grown to a point where build-level project management cannot be fully absorbed alongside those platform responsibilities. As a result, the workflow developer is currently carrying both hands-on development and build-level PM duties simultaneously across the majority of active builds — a structural capacity gap that compounds as the portfolio continues to grow.

Build-Level PM Gap
The Contracts Hub TPM manages platform-wide responsibilities that span the full Hub ecosystem. Individual workflow builds require dedicated build-level project management that currently falls to the workflow developer — limiting bandwidth for both functions and creating compounding risk as the portfolio grows.

Pipeline Backlog

Timeline Slippage Risk

🚨 Potential Engineering Team Transition — Contracts Hub Backend
The Engineering team currently supporting the Contracts Hub backend is potentially being switched. If this transition occurs, it will introduce a ramp-up period during which the incoming team must gain familiarity with the Hub's backend architecture, existing configurations, and active build dependencies. This creates a risk of build freezes, delayed support response, and regression risk across all workflows currently in development or nearing launch.
